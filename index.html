Pentatonic Theory

Minor Pentatonic

The most popular scale in blues, rock, and many other genres. Built from the natural minor scale with the 2nd and 6th degrees removed.

Root, m3, P4, P5, m7

Major Pentatonic

A bright, uplifting scale commonly used in pop, country, and funk. It's the relative major to the minor pentatonic.

Root, M2, M3, P5, M6

CAGED System

The five pentatonic positions correspond to the CAGED chord shapes (C, A, G, E, D). Memorizing these shapes unlocks the entire fretboard.

5 Interconnected Positions

Relative Keys

A minor pentatonic and C major pentatonic share the same notes. Understanding relative keys helps you navigate between modes.

Am pent = C maj pent

Blues Applications

Add the blue note (flat 5) to either pentatonic scale for classic blues sounds. Experiment with bending between notes.

Am + Eb = Blues sound

Practice Tips

Start with one position, then move through all five. Connect positions to develop fluency. Sing the scale to internalize the sound.

Consistency > Speed
