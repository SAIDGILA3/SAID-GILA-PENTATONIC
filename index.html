How the Circle Works

The Circle of Fifths organises the 12 keys by perfect fifth intervals. Moving clockwise adds sharps (or removes flats), moving counter‑clockwise adds flats. Each major key is paired with its relative minor (three semitones lower).

C (0 sharps) → G (1 sharp) → D (2 sharps) …

Pentatonic Connection

Major pentatonic = notes 1, 2, 3, 5, 6 of the major scale. Minor pentatonic = notes 1, 3, 4, 5, 7 of the natural minor scale. The same set of notes works for both a major key and its relative minor. For example, C major pentatonic and A minor pentatonic share identical notes.

C maj pent = A min pent (C, D, E, G, A)

Chromatic Scale

The chromatic scale contains all 12 notes in Western music, each a semitone apart. The tuner detects the frequency of your note and matches it to the nearest chromatic pitch (A4 = 440 Hz).

A, A#, B, C, C#, D, D#, E, F, F#, G, G#

How Pitch Detection Works

The tuner uses autocorrelation to find the fundamental frequency of your instrument. It then calculates the difference in cents (100 cents = 1 semitone) and displays the nearest note name.

f0 = sampleRate / lag_max

Tuning Tips

For guitar, tune each string to the standard EADGBE. Use the tuner to check intonation across the fretboard. Pluck the string clearly and let it sustain for best results.

E2 (82.4 Hz), A2 (110 Hz), D3 (146.8 Hz), G3 (196 Hz), B3 (246.9 Hz), E4 (329.6 Hz)

Pentatonic Theory

Minor Pentatonic

The most popular scale in blues, rock, and many other genres. Built from the natural minor scale with the 2nd and 6th degrees removed.

Root, m3, P4, P5, m7

Major Pentatonic

A bright, uplifting scale commonly used in pop, country, and funk. It's the relative major to the minor pentatonic.

Root, M2, M3, P5, M6

CAGED System

The five pentatonic positions correspond to the CAGED chord shapes (C, A, G, E, D). Memorizing these shapes unlocks the entire fretboard.

5 Interconnected Positions

Relative Keys

A minor pentatonic and C major pentatonic share the same notes. Understanding relative keys helps you navigate between modes.

Am pent = C maj pent

Blues Applications

Add the blue note (flat 5) to either pentatonic scale for classic blues sounds. Experiment with bending between notes.

Am + Eb = Blues sound

Practice Tips

Start with one position, then move through all five. Connect positions to develop fluency. Sing the scale to internalize the sound.

Consistency > Speed
